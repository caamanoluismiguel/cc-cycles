Understanding the Classical Education Model

Welcome to Classical Education

Classical education follows the Trivium - a three-stage learning model that aligns with natural child development. Each stage builds upon the previous, creating a strong foundation for lifelong learning.

The Three Stages of Learning

Grammar Stage
Ages 5-10

Children naturally absorb information like sponges. This stage focuses on building knowledge through memorization and repetition.

Key Focus:

  • Memory work
  • Facts and information
  • Songs and chants
  • Basic skills

Dialectic Stage
Ages 11-13

Students begin asking "why?" and challenging ideas. This stage develops critical thinking and logical reasoning.

Key Focus:

  • Logical thinking
  • Debate and discussion
  • Cause and effect
  • Analysis

Rhetoric Stage
Ages 14-18

Students learn to express knowledge persuasively and beautifully. This stage emphasizes communication and wisdom.

Key Focus:

  • Eloquent expression
  • Written communication
  • Public speaking
  • Original thought
