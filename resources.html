Recommended Reading

Ages 5–7 (Early Grammar Stage) 👶📖

Focus: Foundational stories with biblical themes, moral lessons, and rich language.

  • The Jesus Storybook Bible – Sally Lloyd-Jones
  • A Child’s Garden of Verses – Robert Louis Stevenson
  • Little Pilgrim’s Progress – Helen L. Taylor
  • The Complete Tales of Beatrix Potter – Beatrix Potter
  • Aesop’s Fables – Aesop
  • Winnie-the-Pooh – A.A. Milne
  • Charlotte’s Web – E.B. White

Ages 8–10 (Late Grammar Stage) 🧒📚

Focus: Stronger narratives with Christian virtues, adventure, and wisdom.

  • Dangerous Journey – Oliver Hunkin
  • The Chronicles of Narnia – C.S. Lewis
  • Little House on the Prairie – Laura Ingalls Wilder
  • Missionary Stories with the Millers – Mildred A. Martin
  • The Swiss Family Robinson – Johann Wyss

Ages 11–13 (Dialectic Stage) 👦📖

Focus: Deeper moral complexity, Christian virtue, and discernment.

  • The Pilgrim’s Progress – John Bunyan
  • Treasure Island – Robert Louis Stevenson
  • Anne of Green Gables – L.M. Montgomery
  • The Hobbit – J.R.R. Tolkien
  • Harry Potter (series) – J.K. Rowling

Ages 17–18 (Advanced Rhetoric Stage) 👩‍🎓📚

Focus: Theology, philosophy, apologetics, and literature challenging Christian worldview.

  • The Institutes of the Christian Religion – John Calvin
  • Bondage of the Will – Martin Luther
  • The Abolition of Man – C.S. Lewis
  • 1984 – George Orwell
  • The Brothers Karamazov – Fyodor Dostoevsky

Disclaimer

As Christians, we approach literature with wisdom and discernment, recognizing that while some books contain problematic elements, they also provide valuable insights into human nature, morality, and virtue. As Nancy Pearcey argues in Total Truth, Christians should engage with culture and art critically, rather than avoiding it altogether.

This list is not meant to condemn certain books but to equip readers to think biblically about what they read—avoiding exposure to inappropriate content for their age while appreciating artistry and truth where it can be found.
